# Reference Architecture Comparison: pysystemtrade
Date: 2026-07-14
pysystemtrade commit read: `883c8681cf880d83acad5c39b842403a8eac5676` (2026-06-08), shallow clone, read-only, not installed as a dependency anywhere.

## This Platform's Current Approach

Forecast combination across sleeves does not exist in the live order path today: Trend, MeanReversion, and VRP are each sized and ordered independently (Trend via per-asset vol-targeting in `position_sizer.py`, MR and VRP via hardcoded 10% NAV fractions in `order_engine.py`), with no cross-sleeve signal blending affecting orders. A dashboard-only meta-allocator (`meta_allocator_v0.py`) does combine sleeve-level information — trailing 63-day realized Sharpe ("expected_edge") and a per-sleeve confidence score, cross-sectionally z-scored into a bounded, net-zero tilt around a fixed ERC baseline — but nothing in the order path reads its output. Instrument/sleeve diversification is handled by `portfolio_risk.py`'s `compute_erc_weights()`: true Equal Risk Contribution via Riskfolio-Lib's risk-parity optimizer (full-sample historical covariance, equal risk budgets), replacing an earlier inverse-volatility approximation after a 2026-07-13 peer-review finding of up to 11.75pp divergence — currently observation-mode only (`ERC_LIVE_SIZING=False`). Position scaling targets 11% annualized portfolio volatility in two places: `portfolio_risk.py`'s `portfolio_vol_scalar()` (capped at 2.0x leverage) and, independently, `governance_gate_wiring.py`'s Stage-2 vol-clip (a simpler prior-correlation-matrix book-vol estimate that scales the proposer's *tilt* around ERC, not the ERC weights themselves), plus a third, sleeve-internal vol-target layer inside Trend itself (`position_sizer.py`, 15% per-asset target). Governance is `governance_gates.py`'s three-condition VIX gate (G1 term-structure backwardation F1/F2≥0.95, G2 level spot>200dma, G3 shock +25%/3-day), with asymmetric hysteresis — gates activate immediately on any trigger but deactivate only after two consecutive clear evaluations — driving discrete per-sleeve actions (`suspend`/`reduce_50pct`/`reduce_25pct`/`none`) that `order_engine.py` applies directly for MeanReversion and VRP.

## pysystemtrade's Approach

**1. Forecast combination** (`systems/forecast_combine.py`): a genuine weighted-average blend of multiple trading-rule forecasts *for the same instrument* (e.g. `ewmac8` + `ewmac16` + `carry` for one futures contract) — a different concept than this platform's cross-sleeve blending, closer to how `signal_engine.py` internally combines TSMOM + cross-sectional momentum within the Trend sleeve. Weights are either fixed (config, defaulting to equal 1/N with an explicit warning if unspecified) or estimated via a pluggable optimizer (shrinkage, one-period mean-variance, or bootstrap, all cost-adjusted). Weights are EWMA-smoothed, then a Forecast Diversification Multiplier (FDM) scales the combined forecast back up to compensate for the volatility-reduction from combining imperfectly-correlated rules — either a fixed scalar or estimated from a rolling forecast-correlation matrix. The final forecast is hard-capped (default ±20).

**2. Position sizing / vol targeting** (`systems/positionsizing.py`): each instrument is sized independently to a *single, system-wide* percentage vol target (`percentage_vol_target × notional_trading_capital`), via `vol_scalar = cash_vol_target / instrument_value_vol`. This is a per-instrument vol-targeting layer, methodologically similar to this platform's Trend-internal `position_sizer.py`, but there is no separate, distinct "per-sleeve target vs whole-portfolio target" split the way this platform has (target_sleeve_vol vs target_portfolio_vol) — pysystemtrade targets vol once, at the instrument level, and lets portfolio-level combination (below) determine the aggregate outcome, with an optional long-only constraint and a position-buffering mechanism (band around the target position to reduce unnecessary turnover) that this platform has no equivalent of.

**3. Instrument diversification** (`systems/portfolio.py`): **not ERC/risk-parity by a convex covariance optimization** — instrument weights come from the same pluggable-optimizer framework as forecast weights (fixed/equal, shrinkage, one-period, bootstrap), then an Instrument Diversification Multiplier (IDM), estimated from a rolling instrument-correlation matrix, scales the combined position up to restore the target vol lost to diversification. Checked the actual current default in code (not assumed from the book): `sysdata/config/defaults.yaml` sets `instrument_weight_estimate.method: handcraft` — Carver's "handcrafted" method is the live default, not a superseded book-only historical choice. Read `sysquant/optimisation/handcraft.py`: it computes `risk_weights` via **hierarchical correlation clustering** (recursively splits into pairs rather than solving the full joint covariance matrix, explicitly to stay robust when correlation history is thin), with `equalise_vols=True` by default and `equalise_SR=False` by default — i.e., it deliberately targets equal volatility contribution per instrument/cluster, not a Sharpe-tilted allocation, echoing risk-parity's spirit through a different, more robustness-oriented mechanism than a direct covariance solve.

**4. Risk overlay / governance** (`systems/risk_overlay.py`, wired into `portfolio.py`'s `get_risk_scalar()`): a single continuous multiplier in [0, 1] applied uniformly across all positions, taken as the **minimum** of four independently-computed sub-multipliers: (a) normal portfolio risk vs. a fraction of vol target, (b) *shocked*-volatility portfolio risk (same weights, stressed vol estimate — protects against jumpy, non-stationary vol), (c) sum-of-absolute-risk (a proxy for what risk would be under a correlation breakdown/shock), (d) gross leverage vs. a hard cap. Each sub-multiplier scales down smoothly and proportionally to how far the risk measure exceeds its limit — there is no discrete on/off state, no hysteresis/confirmation-day mechanism visible in this module, and critically, **all four inputs are endogenous** (derived from the system's own estimated risk), with no equivalent of an exogenous market-regime trigger like the VIX gate.

## Points of Agreement

- **Vol targeting as the primary scaling mechanism, not signal refinement**, is the shared foundational premise of both systems — this platform's own module docstring ("volatility scaling contributes more incremental Sharpe than signal refinement") is exactly pysystemtrade's design center of gravity too.
- **Equal risk contribution as the goal for cross-asset/cross-strategy diversification** is validated independently: pysystemtrade's actual current default (`handcraft`, confirmed in code) targets equal volatility contribution per instrument, the same objective this platform's Riskfolio-Lib RP solver targets for sleeves — arrived at via a different mechanism (hierarchical clustering vs. direct covariance optimization), but the same underlying principle. This is real validating evidence for the ERC design choice, not just an internal preference.
- **A risk overlay that can scale the whole book down, separate from and after the diversification/weighting step**, exists in both: pysystemtrade's `risk_scalar` and this platform's VIX gate + vol-clip both sit downstream of weight computation and can override it.
- **Diversification multipliers that account for imperfect correlation** appear at multiple levels in pysystemtrade (FDM at the forecast level, IDM at the instrument level) and conceptually in this platform's `compute_cross_sleeve_correlations()` / redundancy-flagging logic, though this platform doesn't currently use a correlation-based multiplier to scale weights back up the way pysystemtrade's FDM/IDM do.

## Points of Difference

- **Discrete governance actions (this platform) vs. a continuous risk multiplier (pysystemtrade).** This is a deliberate, already-documented design choice here, not an oversight — the VIX gate exists specifically because VRP (a short-vol carry sleeve) has a known, well-understood blowup mode that a smooth continuous scalar would react to too slowly; a hard `suspend` is the intended behavior for that specific risk. pysystemtrade's four-component continuous multiplier is designed for a diversified, no-single-catastrophic-sleeve futures book where gradual scaling is the right response. Neither is objectively better — they fit different sleeve compositions. Worth naming explicitly, though: pysystemtrade's overlay generalizes to *any* correlation shock across the whole book (via the sum-abs-risk and shocked-vol components), whereas the VIX gate is VRP-specific and scoped by `SLEEVE_GATE_RESPONSES` per-sleeve — Trend has no VIX gate exposure at all in the live order path today.
- **Exogenous (VIX-based) vs. endogenous (self-referential) risk triggers.** The VIX gate watches an external market indicator; pysystemtrade's risk overlay watches only its own estimated/realized risk (normal, shocked, correlation-shock proxy, leverage). This platform has no endogenous-risk equivalent — no check for "has this portfolio's *own* estimated vol jumped, independent of any external VIX reading." That's a genuine structural gap worth naming (see below), not an equivalent-but-different design choice.
- **ERC via direct covariance optimization (this platform) vs. hierarchical clustering (pysystemtrade's actual default).** Carver's own codebase chose the clustering approach specifically for robustness when correlation estimates are unreliable with limited history — directly relevant context given this platform's own `compute_erc_weights()` currently falls back to equal weights below a 21-observation threshold, and Trend sleeve history is still thin in production. This is not a "pysystemtrade is right, adopt it" point — Riskfolio-Lib's full covariance solve is more precise when the covariance estimate is trustworthy — but it's worth knowing that a respected reference implementation treats thin-correlation-history robustness as important enough to trade away optimization exactness for.
- **No governance hysteresis/confirmation-day analog found in pysystemtrade's risk overlay.** This platform's 2-day deactivation confirmation (preventing gate whipsaw) has no equivalent in the specific module read here — smoothing in pysystemtrade happens via EWMA on *weights*, not on the risk overlay's own multiplier. Not evidence the VIX gate's hysteresis is wrong; just noting it's this platform's own addition, not something mirrored in the reference.

## Anything Worth Flagging Before Phase E

The clearest actionable gap: this platform's governance layer has no endogenous risk check — nothing watches the portfolio's *own* realized/estimated volatility or correlation structure and scales down independent of the VIX. Since VRP is the only sleeve the VIX gate actually governs today (Trend and MeanReversion's gate exposure is `none`/`reduce_50pct` at most, and Trend has no gate exposure at all in the live order path), a correlation breakdown or vol spike *not* accompanied by a VIX signal — e.g. a Trend/MeanReversion-specific shock — would currently pass through completely ungated. This doesn't need pysystemtrade's exact four-component design, but a simple endogenous overlay (portfolio-level realized vol vs. target, checked independently of VIX) is a reasonable thing to scope for Phase E, alongside — not instead of — the existing VIX gate.
